More About Me

Since you’re reading this part, I assume you want to know more about me. I guess I’ll get a little bit more personal here.

You might have noticed that I use Third Person her to introduce myself on top of this page. I was inspired by my English prof's description of his office. He is a teacher full of personal charm and one of my favorite instructors at UBC.

I like taking photos. I don’t have professional cameras and lens, so all my photos were taken with my 2-year-old phone. As I’m walking around everyday mostly on UBC Vancouver campus, I took a number of pictures of UBC and Vancouver. Taking photos is my way of recording life, as well as a constant reminder that I will not gradually become numb and surrender to life. It is to appreciate and remember all those tiny but beautiful things in life. If you’re interested in seeing my life through my eyes, check out my Instagram. Feel free to take a look at my collection of food pictures here too, if you are not hungry at the moment. Otherwise I don't suggest doing that as my website is not edible.

Oh and, my food collection is from my UBC Blog site. I put some thoughts there from time to time, but mostly photos. The only thing that bothers me at the moment is I'm running out of blog space (84% used!). Some time later maybe I will integrate a blog section into this website.

I'm learning Japanese. Check out my video project for an introductory Japanese course (JAPN 100) here. I used to watch a lot of Japanese dramas, but not anymore as I don’t have that much time. I also watch some animes, surprisingly, with my dad. He is into animes too! Overall, me and my family are just very interested in Japanese culture. I did take a Japanese culture course (ANTH 215) at UBC, although that didn’t end up quite well in terms of how fun it was.

I love Markdown. Markdown is simple, yet so beautiful. Check out some of my notes on GitHub Gist, most of which were written in Markdown using my favorite Markdown editor Typora. I'm also hoping to rebuild this site with Jekyll when I have time.

That’s all for now.
