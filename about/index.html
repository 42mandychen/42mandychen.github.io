To teach is to learn again

From May 2016 to April 2017, I had been an Undergraduate Teaching Assistant for CPSC 110 (Computation, Programs, and Programming) at UBC. I learned a bunch and wrote about it in a blog post.

Embrace diversity

I have been involved in GIRLsmarts4tech for two years and half, first as a logistics facilitator, then as a program coordinator. It is a Computer Science Department outreach program at UBC sponsored by Microsoft and SAP that teaches Grade 6 and 7 girls about Computer Science. The program mission is to spark and sustain interest in technology for girls through fun, interactive activities to cultivate future confident leaders. The vision is a diverse, equitable culture that empowers and fosters future generations of girls to embrace technology. It has been a great learning experience for me.

As a GIRLsmarts4tech coordinator, I have also been a member of UBC CS Department's CODE (Committee for Outreach, Diversity and Equity). I have become more aware of diversity issues in the technology world as long as the efforts made to create and maintain a diverse tech world.
